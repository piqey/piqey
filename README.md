# ![Biography](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/12129071/174826480-7caac5f4-30e1-46f8-b7ba-3f13d220ec64.png)
I'll keep it short. I'm a Half-Life, Stargate & JoJo's Bizarre Adventure superfan, PC enjoyer, Source Engine documentation database and s&dev. I usually work in C#, C++ and Python, though I'm not doing so very actively at this time.

I'm currently in school finishing up my Associate in Arts at State College of Florida, after which I plan on applying to the University of Central Florida or elsewhere to study computer science (as I have been doing for the last 10+ years as a hobby)—all thanks to a [very good friend](https://github.com/ThomasTerp) I met in *Garry's Mod* all those years ago who introduced me to it.

As a result of the intimacy between my self-education in programming and my passion for video game development, I would say the latter would be the ideal career for me. Though it's no mystery that the job market for programming (even without the relation to video games) is more flooded by computer science alumni than ever before, I'm feeling more optimistic than I ever have about my chances at success in a career I would love.

Even if I don't manage to score a career in video game deveopment, I don't think I'd mind any other subject as long as it involved writing software—plain and simple. Whenever there's a problem without a solution, I tend to have a substantial interest. *Maybe not so much when money isn't involved, but that's beside the point.*

As for this provisional autobiography, for the time being it is complete (though more may be soon to come).
